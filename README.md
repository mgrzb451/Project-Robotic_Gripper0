### Stress Results
The Contour Plot limits show the Re of aluminium we used. The stresses in the links, arms and our structural components are very low - great sign with even better implications! 

<img width="1288" height="801" alt="image" src="https://github.com/mgrzb451/Project-Robotic_Gripper0/blob/main/assets/stress0.jpg" />

### Strain Energy Results
We can use the Strain Energy contour plot to guide design optimizations in further iterations. We can see that adding additional stiffeners (in the form of pins for example) in the centers of the Links and towards the rear of the arms 
would greatly stiffen the design without adding much cost or complexity

<img width="1533" height="820" alt="image" src="https://github.com/mgrzb451/Project-Robotic_Gripper0/blob/main/assets/strain_energy0.jpg" />

# Conclusions
### #1
The results seem to show an interesting situation where major individual components are far too strong but the structure itself could use some more rigidity.
This is great news as it show that the design can be vastly improved in terms of light weighting through aggressive material removal.
To address the deformations that we observed additional stiffening elements should be included.
These would ideally be normalized hardware-type elements like additional pins, bushings etc.
They would hardly complicate the design at all and the additional cost will be minimal since that's cheap stuff. Awesome! üòÅ
### #2 
This project shows the incalculable (pun intended üòè) value of simulation driven design. Performing even cursory FEA at early stages of the design process can yield profound insights translating to significant optimizations in the resulting product!
